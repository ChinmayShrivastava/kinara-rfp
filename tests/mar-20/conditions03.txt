- The application will be evaluated on the consideration of new collaborations with foreign entities, organizations, and governments, so the applicant must provide advanced written notification to DOE and await further guidance before contacting the proposed foreign entity.
- The application will be evaluated on existing collaborations with foreign entities, organizations, and governments, so the applicant must provide a written list of all existing foreign collaborations.
- The application will be evaluated on the description of collaborations that should be reported, so the applicant must report all resources made available in connection with the DOE award, including in-kind contributions.
- The application will not be considered if collaborations do not involve provision of a thing of value to or from the recipient, so the applicant must ensure all collaborations meet this criteria.
- The application will not be considered if collaborations do not include routine workshops, conferences, or use of recipient's services and facilities by foreign investigators, so the applicant must ensure reported collaborations meet the criteria.
- The application will be evaluated on Overall FOA Responsiveness and Viability of the Project, so the applicant must address these factors in the Concept Paper.

- The applicant will be evaluated on how clearly they describe the proposed technology, its uniqueness, and innovation, as well as how it will advance the current state-of-the-art, so the applicant must provide detailed and convincing explanations.
- The applicant will be evaluated on their identification of risks, challenges, regulatory and financial aspects, and mitigation strategies, as well as the impact of EERE funding and the proposed project on the relevant field and application, so the applicant must thoroughly address these aspects.
- The applicant will be evaluated on their qualifications, experience, capabilities, and resources necessary to complete the proposed project, so the applicant must demonstrate their readiness and ability to carry out the project.
- The applicant will be evaluated on whether the proposed work would clearly meet the objectives stated in the FOA, so the applicant must align their project goals with the FOA objectives.
- The application will be evaluated on technical merit, innovation, and impact, including factors such as the extent of innovation, clarity in describing advancements, sufficiency of technical detail, consideration of manufacturing and supply chain challenges, environmental constraints, emissions reduction potential, and infrastructure support, so the applicant must address these aspects thoroughly in their application.
- The application will be evaluated on the ability of the project to advance industry adoption, so the applicant must demonstrate a clear impact on industry advancement.
- The application will be evaluated on the extent to which the project supports the topic area objectives and target specifications and metrics, so the applicant must align the project closely with the specified objectives.
- The application will be evaluated on the potential impact of the project on advancing the state of the art, so the applicant must showcase how the project contributes to technological advancement.
- The application will be evaluated on the extent to which demonstration/deployment is replicable and may lead to future demonstrations, so the applicant must provide a detailed plan for replicability and scalability.
- The application will be evaluated on the extent to which the project facilitates stakeholder relationships across new or existing stakeholders, so the applicant must demonstrate the ability to engage stakeholders effectively.

- The application will be evaluated on the adequacy of proposed project management systems, so the applicant must have robust systems in place to track scope, cost, and schedule progress.
- The application will be evaluated on the reasonableness of budget and spend plan, so the applicant must provide a detailed and justified budget plan.
- The application will be evaluated on the adequacy, reasonableness, and soundness of the project schedule, so the applicant must have a well-defined schedule with clear milestones.
- The application will be evaluated on the adequacy of the identification of risks and mitigation strategies, so the applicant must identify potential risks and provide appropriate mitigation plans.

- The application will be evaluated on the degree to which the approach and critical path have been clearly described and thoughtfully considered, so the applicant must provide a detailed and well-thought-out demonstration approach.
- The application will be evaluated on the degree to which the task descriptions are clear, detailed, timely, and reasonable, so the applicant must ensure that the Workplan and SOPO are well-defined and achievable.
- The application will be evaluated on the discussion and demonstrated understanding of key technical risk areas and mitigation strategies, so the applicant must identify technical risks and provide quality mitigation strategies.

- The application will be evaluated on the level of clarity in the definition of the baseline, metrics, and milestones; and relative to a clearly defined project baseline, the strength of the quantifiable metrics, milestones, and mid-point deliverables defined in the application, so the applicant must ensure meaningful interim progress will be made.
- The application will be evaluated on the identification of target market, competitors, and distribution channels for proposed technology along with known or perceived barriers to market penetration, including mitigation plan; and comprehensiveness of market transformation plan including but not limited to product development and/or service plan, commercialization timeline, financing, product marketing, legal/regulatory considerations including intellectual property, infrastructure requirements, Open-source Software Distribution Plan, etc., and product distribution, so the applicant must provide a detailed market transformation plan.
- The application will be evaluated on the identification of the interest and extent of industry adoption of the technology/process, so the applicant must clearly demonstrate industry adoption potential.
- The application will be evaluated on the capability of the project manager(s) and the proposed team to address all aspects of the proposed work with a high probability of success, the qualifications, relevant expertise, and time commitment of the individuals on the team, diversity of expertise and perspectives of the team and the inclusion of industry partners that will amplify impact, provision of appropriate resources in budget to ensure livable wages and benefits for all employees, including students, early career researchers, or other trainees employed on the project, sufficiency of the facilities to support the work, degree to which the proposed consortia/team demonstrates the ability to facilitate and expedite further demonstration, development, and commercial deployment of the proposed technologies, level of participation by project participants as evidenced by letter(s) of commitment and how well they are integrated into the Workplan, and reasonableness of the budget and spend plan for the proposed project and objectives, so the applicant must meet all these criteria to be considered.
- The application will be evaluated on the R&D Community Benefits Plan criterion, which accounts for 15% of the evaluation, so the applicant must address the following factors:
- The application will be evaluated on the clear articulation of the project’s goals related to diversity, equity, inclusion, and accessibility, so the applicant must clearly define these goals.
- The application will be evaluated on the quality of the project’s DEIA goals, including depth, breadth, likelihood of success, inclusion of appropriate and relevant SMART milestones, and overall project integration, so the applicant must ensure high-quality goals.
- The application will be evaluated on the degree of commitment and ability to track progress toward meeting each of the DEIA goals, so the applicant must demonstrate commitment and tracking ability.
- The application will be evaluated on the extent of engagement of organizations that represent disadvantaged communities as a core element of their mission, including Minority Serving Institutions (MSIs), Minority Business Entities, and nonprofit or community-based organizations, so the applicant must engage these organizations.
- The application will be evaluated on the clear workplan tasks, staffing, research, and timeline for engaging energy equity stakeholders and/or evaluating the possible near- and long-term implications of the project for the benefit of the American public, including public health and public prosperity benefits, so the applicant must provide a clear plan for energy equity.
- The application will be evaluated on the approach, methodology, and expertise articulated in the plan for addressing energy equity and justice issues associated with the technology innovation, so the applicant must demonstrate expertise in this area.
- The application will be evaluated on the likelihood that the plan will result in improved understanding of distributional public benefits and costs related to the innovation if successful, so the applicant must show potential for improved understanding.
- The application will be evaluated on the clear and comprehensive workplan tasks, staffing, research, and timeline for engaging workforce stakeholders and/or evaluating the possible near- and long-term implications of the project for advancing and retaining good jobs for the U.S. workforce, so the applicant must provide a detailed plan for engaging the workforce.
- The application will be evaluated on the approach to document the knowledge, skills, and abilities of the workforce required for successful commercial deployment of innovations resulting from this research, so the applicant must document workforce requirements.
- The application will be evaluated on the likelihood that the plan will result in improved understanding of the workforce implications related to the innovation if successful, so the applicant must demonstrate potential for improved understanding.
